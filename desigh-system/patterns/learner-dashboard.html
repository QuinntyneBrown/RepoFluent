Information priority

  1. Recommended next action: one explainable suggestion with title, reason, duration, context, and resume action.
  2. Required assignments: separated from optional enrichment, ordered by due date and urgency.
  3. Learning evidence: completion, objective coverage, latest result, mastery, and recency remain distinguishable.
  4. Recent activity: meaningful completion, score, assignment, and source-version events—not a noisy audit log.

Recommendation rules

Explain why this is next

Examples: “Resume where you stopped,” “Due in two days,” “Review after an incorrect answer,” or “Knowledge is becoming stale.” The learner can choose another permitted item.

Do not imply surveillance, optimize for engagement at the expense of required learning, or hide required work behind a recommendation model.

Responsive order

On phone: greeting and next action, required assignments, mastery explanation, recent activity. Cards become a single column; assignment records retain type, title, date, and state. Nothing required becomes hover-only.
