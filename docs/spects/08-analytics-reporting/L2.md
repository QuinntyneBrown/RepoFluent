## Detailed requirements

### L2-ANR-01 — Canonical metric catalog

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-10, L1-ANR-11

The subsystem shall maintain versioned definitions for assignment status, participation, completion, pass/fail, objective coverage, assessment result, mastery, recency/staleness, confidence, learning time, knowledge gap, and high-performance indicators. Each definition shall identify sources, grain, filters, exclusions, time semantics, null behavior, and owning policy version.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a release containing analytics views and exports
- **WHEN** a reviewer selects any displayed metric
- **THEN** a current definition identifies calculation, evidence sources, time window, grain, exclusions, and version used by both view and export

### L2-ANR-02 — Learner self-reporting view

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-01; ANL-01

The learner view shall report only the authenticated learner’s assignments, state, due status, course/objective completion, assessment results whose feedback is released, objective coverage/mastery, recency, and recommended next actions. It shall link each value to understandable source context where appropriate.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a learner with mixed assignments and one unreleased summative result
- **WHEN** the learner opens analytics
- **THEN** all permitted personal measures are accurate, the unreleased detail remains protected, and no other learner data is returned

### L2-ANR-03 — Manager aggregate dashboard

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-02; ANL-02

Authorized managers shall filter an authorized population by time, curriculum/version, course, system/subsystem, objective, assignment state, and cohort/team. Aggregates shall include participation, completion, mastery distribution, assessment distribution, active learning time, and common gaps with denominators and freshness.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a manager authorized for team Alpha but not team Beta
- **WHEN** the manager opens aggregate analytics and attempts to include both teams
- **THEN** only Alpha contributes data, denominators and freshness are visible, and Beta is not disclosed as a selectable population

### L2-ANR-04 — Drill-down authorization

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-03, L1-ANR-06; ANL-03/06

Every drill-down shall re-evaluate authorization and minimum-group policy at the target grain. A visible aggregate shall not automatically authorize individual detail. Filters shall carry explicit version/population context and shall not be client-trusted.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a manager can see an aggregate for a qualifying cohort but lacks individual-learner permission
- **WHEN** the manager selects a chart segment or directly calls learner drill-down
- **THEN** individual data is denied while permitted aggregate context remains available

### L2-ANR-05 — Learning state classification

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-04; ANL-04

Classification shall apply documented precedence and permit simultaneous orthogonal facts where necessary, such as completed-and-failed or passed-and-stale. Views shall not force these facts into a misleading single state.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an overdue assignment later completed with a failed test and mastery evidence now stale
- **WHEN** the record is reported
- **THEN** completion, failed outcome, prior overdue history, and stale knowledge are represented accurately without one silently erasing another

### L2-ANR-06 — Component learning measures

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-10

Reports answering “how much has been learned” shall present assigned completion, objective coverage, demonstrated mastery, assessment performance/attempt history, recency/staleness, and enabled self-confidence as separate measures with appropriate denominators. No unlabeled blended score shall replace them.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a cohort with high completion but low mastery and stale results
- **WHEN** a manager views system knowledge
- **THEN** the divergent measures remain individually visible and the UI does not imply high demonstrated proficiency from completion alone

### L2-ANR-07 — Knowledge-gap identification

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-02, L1-ANR-10

Knowledge gaps shall be derived from documented evidence such as low/insufficient objective mastery, repeated errors, low coverage, and staleness. A gap shall name the objective/system, population, evidence count, time window, and confidence/limitations and shall link to permitted remediation.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a cohort with repeated event-delivery errors but insufficient evidence for payment processing
- **WHEN** common gaps are calculated
- **THEN** event delivery is identified with its evidence and payment processing remains labeled insufficient evidence rather than low mastery

### L2-ANR-08 — Transparent high-performance indicators

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-05; ANL-05

If enabled by tenant policy, authorized users may identify learners who meet explicitly selected indicators such as required completion, objective coverage, assessment thresholds, mastery recency, or improvement. The interface shall show criteria, time window, evidence, and limitations and shall avoid a hidden ordinal leaderboard.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** the tenant enables an indicator requiring current mastery in three objectives and an 80% latest assessment result
- **WHEN** a manager views qualifying learners
- **THEN** only learners meeting both transparent conditions appear, criteria/evidence are visible, and no unexplained composite rank is produced

### L2-ANR-09 — Minimum-group privacy

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-06; ANL-06

Tenant policy shall define a minimum reporting group within platform bounds. Counts, aggregates, distributions, filters, comparisons, totals, tooltips, exports, and differencing-prone queries shall suppress or coarsen results below the threshold. Authorized individual views shall require a separate explicit grant.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a minimum group of five and a filter that leaves three learners
- **WHEN** a manager requests the chart, table, tooltip, drill-down, and export
- **THEN** every aggregate surface applies consistent suppression and cannot reveal values by subtracting visible totals

### L2-ANR-10 — Report export

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-07; ANL-07

Exports shall use a documented common machine-readable format, stable columns/schema version, applied filters, population/time/version labels, metric-definition versions, generated time, and safe null/suppression representation. Generation, download, expiry, and failure shall be authorized and audited.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a manager exports a filtered permitted report
- **WHEN** the export completes
- **THEN** its rows reconcile to the UI under the same definitions and suppression rules, metadata describes filters/versions/time, and the export event is audited

### L2-ANR-11 — Content-quality signals

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-08; ANL-08

Content-quality reporting should measure defined abandonment, repeated failure, attempt patterns, item difficulty/discrimination where statistically valid, learner feedback, render errors, and remediation usage. It shall display sample size and avoid item discrimination claims below the approved evidence threshold.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a question with too few attempts for valid discrimination analysis and a lesson with a measurable abandonment spike
- **WHEN** a reviewer opens content-quality analytics
- **THEN** the question metric is withheld/labeled insufficient while the lesson signal shows its definition, sample, version, and time window

### L2-ANR-12 — Cohort comparison

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-09; ANL-09

Comparisons should require explicit populations, metric/version definitions, curriculum versions, date windows, and inclusion criteria. Differences in population, assignment, content version, or window shall be labeled prominently. Minimum-group policy applies independently to each side.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** two cohorts assigned different curriculum versions and measured in different windows
- **WHEN** an authorized manager compares them
- **THEN** both version/window/population differences are prominent and the interface does not present the observed difference as a controlled causal conclusion

### L2-ANR-13 — Curriculum-version history

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-03, L1-ANR-11; ANL-03/09

All learning evidence shall aggregate under its originating curriculum/item/calculation versions. Cross-version rollups shall be explicit and shall preserve version breakdown. Retirement or publication of a new version shall not rewrite historic aggregates.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** learners completed versions 1 and 2 of a curriculum with different objective coverage
- **WHEN** an authorized user reports by version and then requests an explicit combined view
- **THEN** version-specific results remain reproducible and the combined view labels both populations and coverage differences

### L2-ANR-14 — Freshness and late events

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-11

Every analytics surface shall provide data freshness appropriate to its use. Late, retried, or corrected source events shall be processed idempotently, recalculate affected aggregates, and preserve correction lineage. Stale pipelines shall be visible and monitored.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a delayed attempt result arrives after a dashboard aggregate was produced
- **WHEN** ingestion processes the event
- **THEN** the affected aggregate updates exactly once, freshness advances, and the prior/result lineage remains reconcilable

### L2-ANR-15 — Metric reconciliation

**Traces to:** L1-ANR-11

The subsystem shall provide repeatable controls that reconcile source assignment/progress/attempt/mastery records to aggregates and exports for a selected tenant, version, population, and period. Material discrepancies shall block metric release or raise an operational incident according to threshold.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a seeded dataset with known assignment and attempt outcomes
- **WHEN** the reconciliation suite calculates UI aggregates and export rows
- **THEN** values and denominators match the source-of-truth expectations exactly or produce an actionable discrepancy with lineage
