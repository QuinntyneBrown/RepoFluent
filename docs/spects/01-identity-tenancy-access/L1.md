# Identity, Tenancy, and Access — L1 Requirements

## Document control

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Subsystem | Identity, Tenancy, and Access (ITA) |
| Status | Draft production requirement |
| Source | PRD 6, 8.1, 10, 11, and pilot acceptance criteria 3/6 |
| Owners | Platform Security and Identity |

## Mission and boundary

The subsystem establishes the authenticated actor, tenant security boundary, organizational membership, and authorization context used by every RepoFluent capability. It owns tenant/user/group/role records, sign-in and session integration, policy evaluation, invitations, provisioning interfaces, and identity-related audit events. It does not own curriculum business rules, analytics calculations, or customer identity-provider administration outside RepoFluent.

## High-level requirements

| L1-ITA-07 | Should | Administrators should be able to configure session, authentication, and periodic access-review policies within supported security bounds. | IAM-07 |

## Quality requirements

- Policy evaluation shall default to deny when tenant context, actor identity, role grants, or resource scope is absent or invalid.
- Identity data and credentials shall be protected in transit and at rest using organization-approved mechanisms.
- Authorization decisions shall be deterministic for the same actor, tenant, resource, action, and policy version.
- Session and access-control user interfaces shall meet the launch accessibility and interaction requirements.
- The subsystem shall not log passwords, secrets, access tokens, assertion contents, or sensitive identity attributes.

## Dependencies and exclusions

- Depends on an approved enterprise identity provider and pilot organizational data.
- Publishes actor, tenant, role, group, and policy context to other subsystems; consumers shall not infer access from client-supplied display data.
- Automated provisioning protocol and exact identity provider remain deployment decisions.
- Human-resources performance management and general enterprise directory management are out of scope.
