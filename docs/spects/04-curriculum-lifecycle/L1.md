## Mission and boundary

The subsystem turns an uploaded contract package into a controlled draft, validates it, supports review and learner-equivalent preview, records approval, publishes an immutable version, and retires content without destroying evidence. It owns package intake state, validation orchestration, review workflow, publication state, version identity, and semantic version comparison. It does not define the package schema or render learning interactions outside preview coordination.

## Lifecycle states

At minimum, the subsystem shall distinguish `received`, `validating`, `validation_failed`, `draft`, `in_review`, `approved`, `published`, `retired`, and `processing_failed`. A state transition shall be atomic and shall reject stale or unauthorized commands.

## Quality requirements

- A representative valid package shall become a publishable preview within ten minutes, excluding human review.
- Uploaded bytes shall be retained or discarded according to tenant retention and incident needs; no active content shall execute.
- Background operations shall be idempotent and correlated across API, worker, audit, and UI status.
- Learners shall never resolve drafts, failed versions, approval artifacts, or retired content beyond documented historical access.

## Dependencies and exclusions

- Depends on Curriculum Input Contract, renderer, access policy, audit, assignment, security scanning, and telemetry.
- Managed repository ingestion and autonomous publication are out of scope.
- Visual authoring is post-MVP and shall not weaken review or immutability rules.
