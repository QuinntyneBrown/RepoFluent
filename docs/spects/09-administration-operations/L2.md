## Detailed requirements

### L2-ATO-01 — Administrative information architecture

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-01

The administration experience shall provide tenant-scoped areas for users, groups, roles/access, curricula/versions, assignments, tenant policy/settings, retention, integrations where enabled, audit access, and support diagnostics. Navigation and API discovery shall show only capabilities authorized for the actor.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an Administrator and an Auditor in the same tenant
- **WHEN** each opens administration and calls representative endpoints
- **THEN** the Administrator receives authorized management controls while the Auditor receives read-only views with no mutating operation exposed or accepted

### L2-ATO-02 — User lifecycle administration

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-01, L1-ATO-07; ADM-01

Authorized administrators shall invite, view, activate, deactivate, and where policy permits restore tenant users. Deactivation shall revoke active access without deleting historical authorship, approval, assignment, attempt, or audit references.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an active user with completed learning and approval history
- **WHEN** an administrator deactivates the user
- **THEN** new sessions/access are denied, historical evidence retains attributable subject identity, and the change is audited

### L2-ATO-03 — Group and role administration

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-01, L1-ATO-07; ADM-01

The UI/API shall manage group membership and role grants using the Identity subsystem policy model, show direct versus inherited provenance, validate scope constraints, and preview loss/gain of access for consequential changes.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an administrator removes a manager’s only team-scoped grant
- **WHEN** the administrator reviews and confirms the change
- **THEN** the UI identifies the affected scope, access is removed atomically, and a before/after audit record is created

### L2-ATO-04 — Curriculum administration

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-01, L1-ATO-02; ADM-01/02

Authorized users shall list curricula by lifecycle state/version, inspect ownership/source snapshot/validation/review/publication data, invoke only permitted lifecycle actions, and identify current assignment use. Administrative actions shall delegate to lifecycle gates rather than altering content state directly.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a draft with blocking validation errors and an Administrator
- **WHEN** the Administrator attempts publication from the curriculum view or API
- **THEN** publication is denied by the lifecycle gate and the blocking issues link to codes and paths

### L2-ATO-05 — Direct and group assignments

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-08; ADM-01, IAM-04

An authorized administrator shall assign a published curriculum/version to users or groups with required/optional state, availability/due dates, and completion policy reference. Derived group assignments shall retain provenance and shall not duplicate an equivalent direct/effective assignment.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a learner belongs to two groups assigned the same version and also has an equivalent direct assignment
- **WHEN** assignment resolution runs
- **THEN** the learner has one effective learning obligation with all assignment provenance preserved and no duplicate progress requirement

### L2-ATO-06 — Assignment update and removal

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-08, L1-ATO-07

Changes to due date, requirement, or audience shall be versioned/audited and shall define impact on started/completed learners. Removing a source group membership or assignment shall not delete learning evidence. The UI shall preview affected population and completed/in-progress counts before broad changes.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a required group assignment with completed and in-progress learners
- **WHEN** an administrator removes it after reviewing the impact preview
- **THEN** future obligation follows policy, existing evidence remains, affected counts reconcile, and the removal is audited

### L2-ATO-07 — Processing and publication status

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-02; ADM-02

Status shall show lifecycle state, active/completed stages, start/update timestamps, validation counts by severity, warning acknowledgement, review decision, publication state, safe error summary, retry availability, and correlation identifier. Updates should occur without requiring a full-page refresh.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an upload fails validation on a cross-reference
- **WHEN** the author opens processing status
- **THEN** the state, blocking count, stable issue code, JSON path, safe explanation, and correlation identifier are visible and no unsupported retry is offered

### L2-ATO-08 — Retention policy configuration

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-03; ADM-03

Authorized administrators shall view/configure supported retention periods for uploaded packages, drafts, published content, learning records, exports, and user-generated artifacts within legal/platform bounds. Effective date, default, exception, and legal-hold precedence shall be explicit.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a tenant default and a legal hold affecting a subset of learning records
- **WHEN** an administrator changes the normal retention period
- **THEN** the effective policy preview excludes held records from deletion eligibility and the policy change is versioned/audited

### L2-ATO-09 — Deletion workflow

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-03, L1-ATO-07; ADM-03

Deletion shall be a tracked workflow with request authorization, scope/impact preview, confirmation, hold and audit constraints, queued execution, derived-store/cache/index handling, per-stage status, verification, and immutable proof of disposition that does not retain deleted payloads.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an authorized deletion request for retired curriculum with no hold and derived search/cache data
- **WHEN** the request is confirmed and completes
- **THEN** eligible primary and derived data are removed according to policy, protected audit/evidence constraints remain satisfied, and a payload-free disposition record identifies scope and outcome

### L2-ATO-10 — Safe support diagnostics

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-04; ADM-04

Tenant diagnostics shall show service status relevant to the tenant, processing job status, recent safe error identifiers, client/browser capability summary where provided, configuration versions, and support correlation data. Source excerpts, answer data, secrets, tokens, stack traces, and unrelated tenant metadata shall be excluded.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a rendering failure involving restricted curriculum content
- **WHEN** an administrator downloads or views support diagnostics
- **THEN** the artifact contains correlation, component, time, safe category, and relevant version but no source excerpt, answer, credential, or other-tenant data

### L2-ATO-11 — Tenant branding

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-05; ADM-05

Branding should support tenant display name, approved logo asset, constrained color tokens, and approved sign-in message. Inputs shall be sanitized and validated for file/type/size, contrast, focus visibility, legibility, and reserved product/security messaging. Invalid branding shall not publish.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an administrator selects brand colors that fail required contrast
- **WHEN** branding validation runs
- **THEN** publication is blocked with the failing token/use and the last valid accessible theme remains active

### L2-ATO-12 — Notification event model

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-06; ADM-06

Notification generation should support assignment created/changed, due-soon/overdue, curriculum publication, and validation failure events. Messages shall be tenant-branded within safety limits, contain minimal data, use authorized deep links, deduplicate retries, and respect channel availability.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a group assignment event is delivered twice by the event bus
- **WHEN** notification processing handles both copies
- **THEN** each affected learner receives at most one notification for the event/channel and its link resolves only after authorization

### L2-ATO-13 — Notification preferences

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-06; ADM-06

Users should configure enabled optional channels and frequencies by notification category. Mandatory security or material administrative notices may be non-disableable and shall be identified. Preferences shall be tenant/user scoped and shall not suppress in-product assignment truth.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a learner disables optional due-date email but retains in-product notices
- **WHEN** a due-soon event occurs
- **THEN** no optional email is sent, the in-product assignment still shows its due state, and mandatory notices remain governed by policy

### L2-ATO-14 — Bulk-operation safety

**Traces to:** L1-ATO-01, L1-ATO-07

Bulk membership, role, assignment, and notification operations shall validate input, preview target counts and exclusions, require confirmation for consequential changes, use an idempotency key, expose per-item outcome, and allow safe retry of failures without duplicating successes.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a bulk assignment for 100 users where 95 are eligible and 5 are unauthorized/ineligible targets
- **WHEN** an administrator confirms and later retries transient failures
- **THEN** 95 receive one effective assignment, 5 have safe per-item reasons, and retries do not duplicate completed work
