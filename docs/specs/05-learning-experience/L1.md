## Mission and boundary

The subsystem delivers assigned and discoverable published curriculum as a coherent learning journey. It owns learner home, course/module/lesson navigation, content composition, progress and resume behavior, curriculum search, contextual glossary, next-step recommendations, review prompts, and private learner artifacts. It consumes code, assessment, and system-map capabilities from their owning subsystems.

## Quality requirements

- Progress writes shall be retry-safe and shall not regress a more advanced durable state because an older client reports late.
- Learner-visible content shall always identify its curriculum version and source snapshot where relevant.
- Draft, retired, inaccessible, and protected-answer content shall not appear in learner search or navigation.
- Recommendations shall be understandable and shall not imply an opaque performance ranking.
- Core flows shall work with keyboard-only input, reduced motion, narrow viewport, and assistive technology.

## Dependencies and exclusions

- Depends on Identity/Access, Curriculum Lifecycle, Code Navigation, Assessment/Mastery, system-map rendering, and analytics event capture.
- Source editing, autonomous code modification, HR learning management, and formal external certification are out of scope.
- Discussion/Q&A is post-MVP unless separately approved.
