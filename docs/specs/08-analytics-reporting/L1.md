# Analytics and Reporting — L1 Requirements

## Document control

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Subsystem | Analytics and Reporting (ANR) |
| Status | Draft production requirement |
| Source | PRD 7.3, 8.8, 10, 11, 13, and pilot acceptance criteria 6/7 |
| Owners | Data Product and Learning Analytics |

## Mission and boundary

The subsystem turns authorized learning evidence into transparent learner and manager views, drill-downs, knowledge-gap signals, curriculum-quality measures, cohort/version comparisons, and exports. It owns metric definitions and query policy, not source evidence creation. It shall support learning improvement and onboarding decisions without becoming an opaque employee ranking system.

## High-level requirements

| ID | Priority | Requirement | PRD trace |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| L1-ANR-01 | Must | Learners shall see their assignments, completion, results, objective coverage, and recommended actions. | ANL-01 |
| L1-ANR-02 | Must | Authorized managers shall see aggregate participation, completion, mastery, result distribution, learning time, and common gaps. | ANL-02 |
| L1-ANR-03 | Must | Authorized analysis shall drill down by curriculum/version, course, system/subsystem, objective, team/cohort, and permitted learner. | ANL-03 |
| L1-ANR-04 | Must | Reporting shall distinguish not started, in progress, completed, passed, failed, overdue, and stale knowledge. | ANL-04 |
| L1-ANR-05 | Must | High-performing learner indicators shall be transparent, configurable, contextual, and shall not use a hidden ranking algorithm. | ANL-05 |

| L1-ANR-07 | Must | Authorized users shall export permitted reports in a common machine-readable format. | ANL-07 |
| L1-ANR-08 | Should | Reporting should expose content-quality signals including abandonment, repeated failure, item discrimination, and learner feedback. | ANL-08 |
| L1-ANR-09 | Should | Cohort and curriculum-version comparisons should clearly label population, version, and time-window differences. | ANL-09 |

## Quality requirements

- All queries and exports shall apply tenant, role, team/cohort, content, and minimum-group policy before returning data.
- Metrics shall remain filterable by curriculum version and cohort; blended populations shall never be implicit.
- Analytics shall preserve historical evidence under the version on which it was earned.
- Small-group suppression shall be applied consistently to charts, tables, tooltips, drill-down, totals, and exports.
- Time-on-learning and activity signals shall not be presented as equivalent to understanding.

## Dependencies and exclusions

- Depends on identity scope, assignments, progress, assessment/mastery, version metadata, event quality, and audit/export services.
- Performance-review scoring, punitive ranking, unrelated learner surveillance, and cross-tenant benchmarking are out of scope.
