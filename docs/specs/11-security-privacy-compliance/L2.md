## Detailed requirements

### L2-SPC-01 — Tenant isolation controls

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-01; IAM-01

Tenant identity shall be server-resolved and propagated through storage, cache, search, queue, object, analytics, export, audit, and telemetry boundaries. Data-access layers shall require tenant context and use tenant-aware keys/partitions/predicates. Background work shall bind to one tenant and reject ambiguous context.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** seeded data for two tenants across primary storage, cache, search, jobs, analytics, and exports
- **WHEN** cross-tenant security tests exercise identifier, filter, queue, cache-key, and direct-object access paths
- **THEN** no content or metadata crosses the boundary and every denied path produces safe attributable evidence

### L2-SPC-02 — Least-privilege service and human access

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-01

Service identities and human operational roles shall receive only the actions/data needed for documented duties, separated by environment and sensitivity. Privileged access shall be approved, time/scoped where supported, authenticated strongly, and audited. Support diagnostics shall not grant implicit curriculum access.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a support operator without explicit restricted-content access
- **WHEN** the operator diagnoses a tenant processing failure
- **THEN** safe metadata/correlation is available but curriculum/source/answers remain denied and any approved elevation follows the audited access workflow

### L2-SPC-03 — Encryption in transit

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-02

All customer/admin/service data shall use organization-approved encrypted transport with certificate/peer validation. Insecure protocols and downgrade paths shall be disabled. Internal exceptions require documented equivalent protection and security approval.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** external and internal endpoints in a production-equivalent environment
- **WHEN** transport-security scanning attempts plaintext, invalid certificate, weak protocol, or downgrade connections
- **THEN** connections fail according to policy and supported encrypted connections succeed

### L2-SPC-04 — Encryption at rest and key management

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-02

Persistent databases, object stores, queues where applicable, backups, indexes, and exports shall use approved at-rest encryption. Key access, rotation, revocation, separation, backup, and failure behavior shall follow organizational policy and shall not embed keys in code/config/logs.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an inventory of production customer-data stores and backups
- **WHEN** the encryption control audit runs
- **THEN** every store maps to an approved key/control, unauthorized key access is denied/audited, and rotation testing preserves availability within recovery objectives

### L2-SPC-05 — Shared-model training prohibition

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-03

RepoFluent shall classify source code, documents, prompts, curriculum, answer keys, learner activity, and analytics as customer data. Product integrations, support tooling, and model/agent providers shall not use it for shared training unless the customer completes an explicit, separate, documented authorization process with defined scope and revocation.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a default tenant with no separate training authorization
- **WHEN** data-flow and provider-configuration controls are inspected
- **THEN** customer data has no shared-training destination or enabled training flag and the default is enforceably off

### L2-SPC-06 — Upload preflight and resource limits

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-04

Before parsing/import, uploads shall enforce authentication, authorization, content length, type/magic checks, archive traversal/bomb protection, entity/nesting/string limits, malware/secret rules as approved, and rate/concurrency limits. Failures shall not create partial domain data.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** malicious fixtures for path traversal, archive expansion, deep nesting, oversized strings, wrong type, and executable content
- **WHEN** intake processes each fixture
- **THEN** each is blocked before domain import within resource budgets and safe logs contain category/correlation but not payload

### L2-SPC-07 — Active-content sanitization

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-04

Renderable curriculum and user-generated content shall use allow-listed structured rendering and context-appropriate output encoding/sanitization. Arbitrary HTML, scripts, event handlers, dangerous URLs, macros, embedded executables, and undeclared remote resources shall not execute or load.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a comprehensive XSS/active-content fixture embedded in every renderable field
- **WHEN** author preview, learner, analytics, admin, notification, and export surfaces process it
- **THEN** no payload executes or loads an unauthorized resource and safe intended text remains correctly encoded

### L2-SPC-08 — Secret detection and response

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-04, L1-SPC-06

Preflight validation shall scan package content and excerpts for configured high-confidence secret patterns and forbidden credential material. A suspected secret shall block publication/intake according to policy, prevent value echoing, and provide secure remediation/incident guidance.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a package containing a seeded high-confidence private key fixture
- **WHEN** scanning runs
- **THEN** the package is blocked, issue/log/UI omit the key value, access is restricted, and the configured security response is triggered

### L2-SPC-09 — Answer-key separation

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-05; ASM-06

Protected answers, grading matchers, and unreleased rationales shall use separate authorization/data transfer models and shall be excluded from learner payloads, static bundles, general search, browser caches where controllable, CDN/public caches, logs, and analytics event properties.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a summative assessment before configured answer release
- **WHEN** an authorized security test inspects learner API responses, client assets/cache, indexes, logs, and telemetry
- **THEN** protected material is absent from every learner-accessible or general-purpose location

### L2-SPC-10 — Source minimization

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-06

Packages and imports shall contain/store only approved excerpts or metadata needed for objectives, explanations, tours, and references. Excerpt length/count and classifications shall be enforceable. When a link/metadata is sufficient, workflows should avoid duplicating whole source files or repositories.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a package containing an entire source file where only a short declared range is referenced and the configured limit is exceeded
- **WHEN** validation runs
- **THEN** publication is blocked or warned per policy with a minimization issue and the system does not silently retain unnecessary source

### L2-SPC-11 — Classification propagation

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-06, L1-SPC-07

Supported content classification/redaction metadata shall propagate from package/source through draft, published content, excerpts, search, analytics, export, cache, logs, backup, and deletion workflows. Derived data shall not receive a less restrictive classification without explicit approved transformation.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a code reference classified Restricted
- **WHEN** it is indexed, rendered, summarized in analytics, and considered for export
- **THEN** each derivative enforces Restricted policy or contains no protected content and retains classification lineage

### L2-SPC-12 — Retention, legal hold, and deletion coverage

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-07; ADM-03

The data inventory shall map each data class to configured retention, hold, deletion, and backup-expiry behavior across primary stores, search/index, caches, analytics derivatives, exports, user artifacts, queues, and replicas. Holds shall prevent prohibited deletion; expiry shall not bypass immutable audit/evidence duties.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** an eligible deletion request with data in primary, search, cache, analytics, export, and backup systems plus one held record
- **WHEN** the workflow completes and backup expiry is later verified
- **THEN** eligible copies are removed according to schedule, the held record remains protected, and disposition evidence covers every inventory location

### L2-SPC-13 — Audit-event integrity

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-08

Audit records shall be append-only/tamper-evident under the selected architecture, tenant-scoped, time-synchronized, access-controlled, queryable by authorized Auditors, and retained under approved policy. Required events shall include administrative access, package upload/validation/review/approval/publication/retirement, assignments, role changes, report exports, retention/deletion, and sensitive content access.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a complete pilot lifecycle including every required event category
- **WHEN** audit conformance and tamper tests execute
- **THEN** events are attributable/correlated and queryable, unauthorized alteration/deletion is prevented or detected, and payload fields follow minimization policy

### L2-SPC-14 — Log and diagnostic redaction

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-09

Structured logging and diagnostics shall use allow-listed fields or centralized redaction to exclude source excerpts, secrets, protected answers, access/refresh tokens, assertion bodies, sensitive free text, and unnecessary personal data. Error values from untrusted content shall be bounded/encoded.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** seeded secrets, source snippets, answers, tokens, and free text pass through failure paths in each subsystem
- **WHEN** test logs, traces, metrics, audit, and support bundles are inspected
- **THEN** forbidden values are absent and required safe correlation and error-category fields remain usable

### L2-SPC-15 — Threat model and privacy review

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-10

Before production customer code is processed, an approved threat model and privacy review shall cover trust boundaries, tenants, identity, agent/package supply chain, uploads, rendering, answer protection, analytics privacy, integrations, operational access, retention/deletion, abuse cases, and mitigations. Material design changes shall trigger review.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** the production architecture and data-flow inventory
- **WHEN** the security/privacy launch gate is reviewed
- **THEN** threats/data uses/risks/controls/owners are documented, critical findings are closed or formally excepted with expiry, and approval is recorded

### L2-SPC-16 — Vulnerability and dependency management

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-10

Build/release processes shall inventory dependencies, scan code/artifacts/configuration, verify provenance where supported, triage findings by policy, patch within severity targets, and prevent release for prohibited unresolved findings. Security testing shall include the launch-critical authorization and content-safety paths.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** a release candidate containing a seeded policy-prohibited dependency vulnerability
- **WHEN** the security release pipeline runs
- **THEN** release is blocked with dependency/version/evidence, an owner is assigned, and no exception occurs without approved scope and expiry

### L2-SPC-17 — Identifier boundary protection

**Traces to:** L1-SPC-11; PRD 10

Package-supplied stable identifiers shall remain namespaced under platform tenant/package/version records and shall never be used directly as global authorization or storage ownership keys. Collisions shall not overwrite entities across packages, versions, or tenants.

**Acceptance criteria**

- **GIVE** two tenants upload packages using identical package and entity identifiers
- **WHEN** both are validated/imported/published
- **THEN** each tenant receives separate platform records and neither can resolve, overwrite, or affect the other
